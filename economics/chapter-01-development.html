Core Concepts

  • Development has different meanings for different people based on their aspirations and needs.
  • Income is one indicator, but not the only one. Health, education, security equally important.
  • National income: total value of goods and services produced. Per Capita Income = National Income / Population.
  • World Bank classification: Rich countries (per capita income > $12,056/year), Low-income (<$1,035).
  • HDI (Human Development Index) by UNDP: combines income + education (literacy, enrollment) + health (life expectancy).
  • India HDI rank: ~132 (medium category). Sri Lanka and Kerala perform better on HDI despite lower income.
  • Sustainability: using resources without compromising future generations. Groundwater depletion is a concern.
  • Public facilities (schools, hospitals, sanitation) crucial — market alone cannot provide for all.

Key Facts & Figures

  • Per Capita Income = National Income ÷ Population
  • HDI = Income index + Education index + Health index
  • World Bank rich country: > $12,056 per capita income/year

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Thinking higher income = more developed (Kerala has lower income but better HDI than Punjab)
  • Confusing National Income with Per Capita Income
  • Forgetting non-material aspects of development

Worked Examples

Example 1: Why is per capita income not a sufficient indicator of development?

Expected answer: It hides inequality, ignores health, education, and non-material factors like freedom and security

Solution: Two countries with same per capita income can differ widely: one may have good schools and hospitals, the other may not. Income average hides rich-poor gap.

Example 2: What is HDI and why is it useful?

Expected answer: Human Development Index combines income, education (literacy), and health (life expectancy) for holistic comparison

Solution: Developed by UNDP. Goes beyond just income. A country with high income but poor health (like some Gulf states) ranks lower. Sri Lanka ranks higher than India despite lower income.

Practice Questions

EASY Q1: What is per capita income?
Answer: National income divided by total population — average income per person.
EASY Q2: What does HDI stand for?
Answer: Human Development Index
EASY Q3: Which organisation publishes HDI?
Answer: UNDP (United Nations Development Programme)
MED Q4: Why does Kerala rank higher on HDI than Punjab?
Answer: Better literacy rate, lower infant mortality, higher life expectancy despite lower per capita income.
MED Q5: What is sustainable development?
Answer: Development that meets present needs without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their own needs.
MED Q6: Why are public facilities important?
Answer: Markets may not provide essential services to the poor. Schools, hospitals, water supply need government involvement for equity.
HARD Q7: Can a country with high income still be "underdeveloped"? Explain.
Answer: Yes — if income is concentrated (inequality), or health/education are poor. Gulf countries may have high income but lower HDI due to education/gender gaps.
HARD Q8: How does groundwater depletion illustrate the need for sustainability?
Answer: Over-extraction exceeds recharge rate. Future generations may not have water. Need to balance current use with long-term availability.
HARD Q9: Compare the World Bank and UNDP methods of measuring development.
Answer: World Bank: uses per capita income only. UNDP: uses HDI (income + education + health). HDI gives a more comprehensive picture.
