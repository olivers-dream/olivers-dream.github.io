Core Concepts

  • Globalisation: integration of economies through trade, investment, technology, and movement of people.
  • MNCs (Multinational Corporations): produce in multiple countries. Set up where cheap labour, resources, government policies are favourable.
  • MNCs invest (FDI — Foreign Direct Investment) or partner with local companies or buy them out.
  • Factors enabling globalisation: technology (internet, containers, fast transport), liberalisation of trade policies.
  • Liberalisation (1991): India removed trade barriers, reduced tariffs, allowed foreign investment — New Economic Policy.
  • WTO (World Trade Organisation): makes rules for international trade. Critics say it favours rich nations.
  • Winners: IT industry, urban consumers (cheaper goods, more variety), large Indian firms going global.
  • Losers: small manufacturers (can't compete), farmers (cheap imports), workers (uncertain jobs, low wages).
  • Fair globalisation: government should protect workers (labour laws), support small producers, negotiate better at WTO.

Key Facts & Figures

  • India liberalised: 1991
  • WTO established: 1995
  • FDI = Foreign Direct Investment
  • SEZ = Special Economic Zone

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Thinking globalisation only means trade — it includes investment, technology, culture
  • Assuming all globalisation is good or all is bad — it creates winners and losers
  • Confusing FDI with trade

Worked Examples

Example 1: How do MNCs spread production across countries?

Expected answer: Set up factories where labour is cheap, partner with local firms, buy out local companies

Solution: MNC headquarters in rich country. Production units in developing countries (cheap labour, raw materials). May buy controlling stake in local firms. Supply chain spans multiple countries. Example: Apple designs in USA, manufactures in China.

Example 2: What was India's liberalisation policy of 1991?

Expected answer: Removed trade barriers, cut tariffs, allowed foreign investment, opened Indian market to global competition

Solution: Before 1991: government controlled imports (high tariffs, quotas). 1991 balance of payments crisis. New Economic Policy: reduced barriers, privatised industries, allowed MNCs. Led to growth but also job losses in protected sectors.

Practice Questions

EASY Q1: What is an MNC?
Answer: Multinational Corporation — a company that operates in more than one country.
EASY Q2: When did India liberalise its economy?
Answer: 1991
EASY Q3: What is FDI?
Answer: Foreign Direct Investment — investment by MNCs by buying property, equipment or setting up production in another country.
MED Q4: How does technology help globalisation?
Answer: Internet enables instant communication, containers standardise shipping, fast transport reduces time, IT enables service exports.
MED Q5: What is WTO and why is it controversial?
Answer: World Trade Organisation makes global trade rules. Controversial because rich countries still protect their farmers while forcing others to open markets.
MED Q6: Give an example of how globalisation helped Indian industry.
Answer: IT companies like TCS, Infosys export software services globally. Indian auto parts firms supply to MNC car makers.
HARD Q7: How has globalisation affected small producers in India?
Answer: Struggle to compete with cheap imported goods. Battery makers, toy makers, cycle makers hit by Chinese imports. Need government support to survive.
HARD Q8: Is WTO truly "free and fair" trade? Discuss.
Answer: Not fully fair: rich nations subsidise their farmers (USA, EU) while demanding developing nations open markets. Rules favour powerful economies.
HARD Q9: What can governments do for fair globalisation?
Answer: Enforce labour laws, support small industries, negotiate better WTO terms, invest in education/infrastructure, ensure safety nets for displaced workers.
