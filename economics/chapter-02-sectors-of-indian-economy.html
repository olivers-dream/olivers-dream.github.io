Central concept connects to main topics.

Core Concepts

  • Primary sector: agriculture, mining, fishing — directly from nature. Also called agriculture sector.
  • Secondary sector: manufacturing, construction — transforms raw materials. Also called industrial sector.
  • Tertiary sector: services — transport, banking, IT, trade. Also called service sector.
  • As economies develop, share of primary decreases, tertiary increases (structural change).
  • India: tertiary sector now largest share of GDP (~54%), but primary still employs most people (~42%).
  • Underemployment (disguised unemployment): more people working than needed. Common in agriculture.
  • Organised vs Unorganised sector: organised has job security, benefits. Unorganised has no protection.
  • MGNREGA (2005): guarantees 100 days of work per year to rural households. If no work, get allowance.
  • Public sector (government) vs Private sector. Public sector important for essential services.

Key Facts & Figures

  • GDP = Primary + Secondary + Tertiary output
  • MGNREGA: 100 days guaranteed work
  • Tertiary sector: ~54% of India's GDP

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Confusing GDP share with employment share — tertiary tops GDP but primary employs more
  • Mixing up organised and unorganised sectors
  • Thinking secondary always grows before tertiary

Worked Examples

Example 1: What is disguised unemployment?

Expected answer: More people employed in a task than needed — removing some wouldn't reduce output

Solution: Common in agriculture: 5 family members work on land that needs only 3. Extra 2 add no output. They are "disguisedly" unemployed.

Example 2: Why has tertiary sector become so important?

Expected answer: Development increases demand for services: transport, banking, IT, healthcare, education

Solution: As primary and secondary grow, they need services (transport, trade, banking). Rising incomes increase demand for education, healthcare, entertainment. IT boom in India exports services globally.

Practice Questions

EASY Q1: Name the three sectors of the economy.
Answer: Primary (agriculture), Secondary (manufacturing), Tertiary (services)
EASY Q2: What is MGNREGA?
Answer: Mahatma Gandhi National Rural Employment Guarantee Act — guarantees 100 days of work per year
EASY Q3: What is GDP?
Answer: Gross Domestic Product — total value of goods and services produced in a country in a year.
MED Q4: Why does primary sector employ most but contribute less to GDP?
Answer: Low productivity per worker, small farms, traditional methods. Services use technology, generate more value per worker.
MED Q5: What is the unorganised sector?
Answer: Small, scattered units with no fixed rules. Workers get no job security, paid leave, or benefits. E.g., street vendors, domestic workers.
MED Q6: How has the share of three sectors changed in India?
Answer: Primary share declined (from ~50% to ~14% of GDP). Tertiary grew to ~54%. Manufacturing stayed ~25%. But primary still employs ~42%.
HARD Q7: Should the government create more jobs in the organised sector?
Answer: Yes — organised sector provides security, regular income, benefits. But also need to protect unorganised workers through laws and social security.
HARD Q8: How can underemployment be reduced?
Answer: Develop secondary/tertiary sectors, improve agricultural productivity (technology, irrigation), MGNREGA-type schemes, skill training.
HARD Q9: Compare public and private sectors with examples.
Answer: Public: government-owned (railways, BSNL), aims for welfare. Private: profit-driven (Reliance, TCS), aims for efficiency. Both needed.
