Book Chapter Summary (Easy Language)

This theorem-based chapter focuses on tangents and circle properties used in proofs and constructions.

  • Differentiate tangent, secant, and chord clearly.
  • Apply tangent-radius perpendicular property in proofs.
  • Use equal tangent theorem from external point repeatedly.
  • Structure geometry proof with marked equal radii and right angles.
💡 Remember It Like This: Touching a soap bubble with finger at exactly one point.

Key Points You Must Know

  • Tangent touches circle at one point.
  • Radius to point of contact is perpendicular to tangent.
  • Tangents from external point are equal.
  • Many proofs use congruent triangles.

Formula / Theorem Quick Box

  • PA = PB (from same external point P to contact points A,B)

Note to Remember

  • Tangent touches at one point only.
  • Radius at contact is always perpendicular.
  • Tangents from same external point are equal.

Worked Examples (Attempt First, Then Reveal)

Example 1: Angle between radius and tangent at contact point?

Expected final answer format: 90

Step-by-step solution: By circle theorem, tangent at any point is perpendicular to radius through that point.

Example 2: From external point P, tangents PA and PB are drawn. Relation?

Expected final answer format: PA=PB

Step-by-step solution: Using congruent triangles OPA and OPB (RHS), PA = PB.

Practice with Solutions

EASY Q1: Tangents from point on circle?
Solution: One tangent
EASY Q2: Can a line intersect circle at 3 points?
Solution: No, maximum 2 points.
EASY Q3: Angle between radius and tangent at contact point?
Solution: 90°
MED Q4: If one tangent length is 9 cm, other?
Solution: 9 cm
MED Q5: From external point P, 2 tangents to circle. What do you call this point?
Solution: External point / Outside the circle
MED Q6: If PA and PB are tangents to circle with center O, what is angle OPA + angle OBP?
Solution: 90°
HARD Q7: Proof idea for equal tangents.
Solution: Use radii and RHS congruence.
HARD Q8: Tangent length from (12, 5) to circle x²+y²=100 is?
Solution: 13
HARD Q9: Two circles with tangent length 15 cm between them. If radii are 12 and 5, find distance between centers.
Solution: 20 cm (external) or 2 cm (internal)
