Book Chapter Summary (Easy Language)

Extends circle geometry into area and perimeter of sectors and segments. Important for mensuration and application sums.

  • Revise circle area and circumference as foundation.
  • Compute arc length and sector area using angle fraction.
  • Find segment area by subtracting triangle from sector.
  • Handle ring (annulus) and mixed-figure mensuration carefully.

Key Points You Must Know

  • Circle area and circumference basics.
  • Sector area is angle fraction of full circle area.
  • Segment area = sector area − triangle area.
  • Ring area uses difference of two circles.

Formula / Theorem Quick Box

  • Area circle = πr²
  • Circumference = 2πr
  • Area sector = (θ/360)πr²

Note to Remember

  • Sector uses angle fraction θ/360.
  • Segment = sector - triangle.
  • Keep units consistent for area.

Worked Examples (Attempt First, Then Reveal)

Example 1: Area of circle radius 7 cm (π=22/7).

Expected final answer format: 154

Step-by-step solution: Area = πr² = (22/7)×49 = 154 cm².

Example 2: Area of 90° sector radius 14 cm.

Expected final answer format: 154

Step-by-step solution: (90/360)×π×14² = 1/4×π×196 = 49π = 154 cm².
