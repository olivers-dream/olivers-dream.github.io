Book Chapter Summary (Easy Language)

Quadratic equations model area, motion and product-based problems. You solve by factorization, completing square and formula, and use discriminant to classify roots.

  • Identify quadratic form and rearrange equations into ax²+bx+c=0 properly.
  • Solve by factorization where possible and formula method for all cases.
  • Use discriminant to predict root nature before solving.
  • Apply quadratics in geometry, area, and consecutive number problems.
💡 Remember It Like This: Ball thrown up traces a parabola; times when it touches ground are roots.

Key Points You Must Know

  • Standard form: ax²+bx+c=0, a≠0.
  • Discriminant D=b²−4ac decides nature of roots.
  • Factorization is fastest when factors are obvious.
  • Formula method always works.

Formula / Theorem Quick Box

  • x = [-b ± √(b²−4ac)] / 2a
  • D = b²−4ac

Note to Remember

  • Write in standard form first.
  • Don't drop ± in formula.
  • Discriminant first saves time.

Worked Examples (Attempt First, Then Reveal)

Example 1: Solve x² - 7x + 12 = 0.

Expected final answer format: 3,4

Step-by-step solution: (x-3)(x-4)=0, so x=3 or x=4.

Example 2: For 2x² + 4x + 5 = 0, what is nature of roots?

Expected final answer format: No real roots

Step-by-step solution: D = 4² - 4·2·5 = -24 < 0, so no real roots.
