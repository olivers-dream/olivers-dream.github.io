Book Chapter Summary (Easy Language)

Probability quantifies chance from 0 to 1. You use favorable outcomes / total outcomes for equally likely events.

  • Define experiment, outcome, event, and sample space clearly.
  • Apply favorable/total rule only for equally likely outcomes.
  • Use complement relation P(E)+P(not E)=1 for shortcuts.
  • Practice dice, cards, and coin events with careful counting.
💡 Remember It Like This: Weather forecast chance — 0 means impossible, 1 means sure.

Key Points You Must Know

  • Probability never less than 0 or more than 1.
  • Certain event has probability 1.
  • Impossible event has probability 0.
  • Experimental probability approaches theoretical over many trials.

Formula / Theorem Quick Box

  • P(E) = Number of favorable outcomes / Total outcomes

Note to Remember

  • Probability range is 0 to 1.
  • Use equally likely outcomes rule carefully.
  • P(E)+P(not E)=1 shortcut.

Worked Examples (Attempt First, Then Reveal)

Example 1: Probability of head on fair coin?

Expected final answer format: 1/2

Step-by-step solution: Favorable outcomes = 1 (H), total = 2 (H,T). So P(H)=1/2.

Example 2: Probability of prime on die?

Expected final answer format: 1/2

Step-by-step solution: Primes on die: 2,3,5 (3 outcomes), total 6, so 3/6=1/2.

EASY Q1: P(even number on die)?
Solution: 1/2
EASY Q2: P(rolling 1 on die)?
Solution: 1/6
EASY Q3: Can probability be 1.5?
Solution: No, always 0 ≤ P(E) ≤ 1
MED Q4: Bag with 5R,3B,2G. P(blue)?
Solution: 3/10
MED Q5: Cards 1 to 20. P(multiple of 5)?
Solution: 1/5
MED Q6: Outcomes when 2 coins are tossed.
Solution: HH, HT, TH, TT (4 outcomes)
HARD Q7: Two coins. P(at least one head)?
Solution: 3/4
HARD Q8: Card from 52. P(red king or queen)?
Solution: 4/52 = 1/13
HARD Q9: Die + coin together. Total equally likely outcomes.
Solution: 12 (6 die × 2 coin outcomes)
