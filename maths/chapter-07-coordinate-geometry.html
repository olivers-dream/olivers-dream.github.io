Key Points You Must Know

  • Distance formula from Pythagoras.
  • Midpoint is average of coordinates.
  • Section formula gives dividing point in ratio.
  • Useful for proving collinearity.

Formula / Theorem Quick Box

  • Distance = √[(x2−x1)² + (y2−y1)²]
  • Midpoint = ((x1+x2)/2, (y1+y2)/2)
  • Section: ((mx2+nx1)/(m+n), (my2+ny1)/(m+n))

Note to Remember

  • Coordinate order is (x,y).
  • Use brackets while subtracting negatives.
  • Section ratio order matters.

Worked Examples (Attempt First, Then Reveal)

Example 1: Distance between (2,3) and (8,11).

Expected final answer format: 10

Step-by-step solution: √[(8−2)² + (11−3)²] = √(36+64)=10.

Example 2: Midpoint of (1,5) and (7,9).

Expected final answer format: (4,7)

Step-by-step solution: ((1+7)/2,(5+9)/2)=(4,7).
