Book Chapter Summary (Easy Language)

This chapter explains how two linear equations can have one, none, or infinitely many solutions. You master elimination, substitution, cross-multiplication and graph interpretation.

  • Represent each equation as a line and read solution type from line behavior.
  • Use consistency ratios to decide one/no/infinite solution before solving.
  • Master substitution, elimination, and cross multiplication method selection.
  • Convert word problems into two variables and validate final ordered pair.
💡 Remember It Like This: Two clues in a detective puzzle point to one person (unique), no person (inconsistent), or many people (dependent).

Key Points You Must Know

  • Intersecting lines ⇒ unique solution.
  • Parallel lines ⇒ no solution.
  • Same line ⇒ infinitely many solutions.
  • Word problems convert to two equations.

Formula / Theorem Quick Box

  • a1/a2 ≠ b1/b2 ⇒ unique solution
  • a1/a2 = b1/b2 ≠ c1/c2 ⇒ no solution
  • a1/a2 = b1/b2 = c1/c2 ⇒ infinitely many solutions

Note to Remember

  • Check ratio conditions before solving.
  • Keep variable meaning fixed in word problems.
  • Verify in both equations.

Worked Examples (Attempt First, Then Reveal)

Example 1: Solve x + y = 9 and x - y = 1.

Expected final answer format: x=5,y=4

Step-by-step solution: Add equations: 2x=10 so x=5. Put in x+y=9 gives y=4.

Example 2: 2x+3y=7 and 4x+6y=14 have how many solutions?

Expected final answer format: Infinite

Step-by-step solution: Second equation is exactly 2× first; both represent same line ⇒ infinitely many solutions.

Practice with Solutions

EASY Q1: Solve 3x+y=11 and x+y=7.
Solution: x=2,y=5.
EASY Q2: Do x+y=5 and 2x+2y=10 have unique solution?
Solution: No, infinitely many
EASY Q3: Are x+y=1 and 2x+2y=3 consistent?
Solution: No, no solution
MED Q4: Find k for infinite solutions: 2x+3y=5 and 4x+6y=k.
Solution: k=10
MED Q5: Solve by elimination: 3x+2y=17 and 2x+3y=18.
Solution: x=3,y=4
MED Q6: Using graph method concept, describe three cases of solution type.
Solution: Unique (intersecting), None (parallel), Infinite (same line)
HARD Q7: Boat speed problem downstream/upstream.
Solution: Set two equations in still-water and stream speeds and solve.
HARD Q8: For 2x+ay=7 and 4x+2y=14 to have infinite solutions, find a.
Solution: a=1
HARD Q9: Age problem: Father is 3 times son's age, in 5 years only 2.5 times. Ages?
Solution: Father=45, Son=15
