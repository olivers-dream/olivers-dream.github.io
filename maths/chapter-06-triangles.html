Key Points You Must Know

  • Similarity criteria: AA, SAS, SSS.
  • Corresponding sides are proportional.
  • Area ratio of similar triangles equals square of side ratio.
  • Pythagoras used in right-triangle applications.

Formula / Theorem Quick Box

  • If Δ1~Δ2 and side ratio = k, then area ratio = k²

Note to Remember

  • Match corresponding vertices in order.
  • Area ratio = square of side ratio.
  • Mark equal angles in proof diagram.

Worked Examples (Attempt First, Then Reveal)

Example 1: If AB/PQ = 2/3 for similar triangles, find area ratio.

Expected final answer format: 4/9

Step-by-step solution: Area ratio = (AB/PQ)² = (2/3)² = 4/9.

Example 2: Right triangle with legs 6 and 8. Hypotenuse?

Expected final answer format: 10

Step-by-step solution: By Pythagoras, c=√(6²+8²)=√100=10.

Practice with Solutions

EASY Q1: Name one similarity criterion.
Solution: AA or SAS or SSS
EASY Q2: Are all equilateral triangles similar?
Solution: Yes
EASY Q3: If side ratio of 2 similar triangles is 3:4, area ratio?
Solution: 9:16
MED Q4: If perimeter ratio is 2:3, side ratio?
Solution: 2:3
MED Q5: Angle bisector theorem: definition/statement.
Solution: Bisector divides opposite side in ratio of adjacent sides.
MED Q6: Basic proportionality theorem (Thales) statement.
Solution: Line parallel to one side divides other two sides proportionally.
HARD Q7: Two triangles have angles 50°,60°,70° and 70°,50°,60°. Similar?
Solution: Yes (AA criterion)
HARD Q8: Angle bisector theorem proof idea.
Solution: Use triangle similarity and proportionality.
HARD Q9: If triangles ABC and DEF have AB/DE=BC/EF=5/7, but ∠B≠∠E. How are they related?
Solution: Not necessarily similar (need one angle equal)
