Book Chapter Summary (Easy Language)

Applies trigonometric ratios to practical measurements like heights of towers and distances using angles of elevation/depression.

  • Translate elevation/depression text into accurate right-triangle diagrams.
  • Select tan/sin/cos based on known and unknown sides.
  • Solve one-step and two-step height-distance applications.
  • Check units and interpretation of final practical answer.

Key Points You Must Know

  • Translate word problem into right triangle.
  • Use tan mostly when height and base are involved.
  • Choose sin/cos when hypotenuse known.
  • Draw labeled diagram first for every sum.

Formula / Theorem Quick Box

  • tanθ = height/base
  • sinθ = height/hypotenuse
  • cosθ = base/hypotenuse

Note to Remember

  • Draw diagram before equation.
  • Elevation is above horizontal.
  • Choose tan when height-base pair appears.

Worked Examples (Attempt First, Then Reveal)

Example 1: From 20 m away, angle elevation is 45°. Height?

Expected final answer format: 20

Step-by-step solution: tan45 = h/20 = 1 ⇒ h=20 m.

Example 2: Kite string 60 m at 30° elevation. Height?

Expected final answer format: 30

Step-by-step solution: h = 60 sin30 = 60×1/2 = 30 m.

Practice with Solutions

EASY Q1: Define angle of elevation.
Solution: Angle above horizontal to object.
EASY Q2: At distance 15 m, angle elevation is 45°. Height?
Solution: 15 m
EASY Q3: What is angle of depression?
Solution: Angle below horizontal when looking down.
MED Q4: Pole shadow 10 m, sun 60°. Height?
Solution: 10√3 m
MED Q5: Ladder of length 13 m leans on wall with base 5 m away. Height reached?
Solution: 12 m
MED Q6: From top of 20 m high building, angle depression to ground object is 30°. Distance from building?
Solution: 20√3 m
HARD Q7: Two points 50 m apart observe top at 30°,60°.
Solution: Height = 25√3 m
HARD Q8: Kite string 100 m at 45° elevation. Horizontal and vertical distances?
Solution: Both = 50√2 m
HARD Q9: Two buildings: From top of first (50m), angle depression to base of second is 30°. From top of second (h), angle elevation to first top is 45°. Find h.
Solution: h = 50(√3-1) m
