Chapter Summary

Understand why and how power is shared in democracies. Study the Belgium and Sri Lanka models, and learn about horizontal and vertical division of power.

  • Belgium model vs Sri Lanka's majoritarianism
  • Horizontal division: legislature, executive, judiciary
  • Vertical division: central, state, local governments
  • Power sharing among social groups and parties
💡 Remember It Like This: Power sharing is like a team project — if one person controls everything, others revolt. Sharing roles makes the team stronger.

Core Concepts

  • Belgium model: power shared among Dutch, French, German-speaking communities through community government.
  • Sri Lanka: majoritarianism by Sinhala majority led to civil war with Tamil minority.
  • Power sharing prevents conflict, ensures stability, and is the spirit of democracy.
  • Horizontal division: among legislature, executive, judiciary (checks and balances).
  • Vertical division: among central, state, and local governments (federalism).
  • Power shared among social groups (community government in Belgium).
  • Power shared among political parties, pressure groups, and movements.

Key Facts & Articles

  • Horizontal = Legislature + Executive + Judiciary
  • Vertical = Central + State + Local

Worked Examples

Example 1: Why did Sri Lanka face civil war?

Expected answer: Majoritarianism: Sinhala-only policies alienated Tamil minority

Solution: Sinhala Only Act 1956, state religion Buddhism, university preference. Tamils felt excluded, leading to ethnic conflict.

Example 2: How did Belgium solve its ethnic problem?

Expected answer: Community government model sharing power among Dutch, French and German speakers

Solution: Equal ministers in central govt, community govts for cultural matters, Brussels separate region with equal representation.

Practice Questions

EASY Q1: What is majoritarianism?
Answer: Rule by the majority community in ways that dominate the minority
EASY Q2: Name two forms of power sharing.
Answer: Horizontal and vertical division of power
EASY Q3: What is checks and balances?
Answer: Each organ of government can check the others, preventing concentration of power.
MED Q4: How is the Belgian model different from Sri Lankan?
Answer: Belgium: accommodation, community govt. Sri Lanka: Sinhala majoritarianism excluding Tamils.
MED Q5: Why is power sharing desirable?
Answer: Reduces conflict, ensures stability, is the spirit of democracy, gives voice to all groups.
MED Q6: What is a community government?
Answer: Elected body by one language community with power over cultural, educational, language matters.
HARD Q7: Is power sharing only about preventing tyranny?
Answer: No. It is also a moral/democratic principle — people have the right to be consulted in governance.
HARD Q8: How do pressure groups share power?
Answer: They influence government decisions through campaigns, protests, lobbying — an informal power sharing mechanism.
HARD Q9: Compare federation and unitary government.
Answer: Federation: power divided between centre and states. Unitary: all power with central government.
