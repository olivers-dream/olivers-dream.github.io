Core Concepts

  • Democracy produces accountable, responsive, and legitimate government.
  • Democracies are better at handling differences and conflicts peacefully.
  • Economic growth: democracies have slightly lower growth rate than dictatorships, but more sustainable.
  • Democracy reduces inequality through welfare schemes, but economic inequality persists.
  • Democracy ensures dignity of citizens: women, minorities, lower castes get constitutional rights.
  • Democracy allows people to correct mistakes through elections.
  • Transparency: RTI Act (2005) empowers citizens to question government.
  • Complaints about democracy indicate success — people have high expectations from democracy.

Key Facts & Articles

  • RTI Act: 2005 — Right to access government information
  • Democracy = Accountability + Transparency + Dignity

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Thinking democracy guarantees economic growth
  • Confusing legitimacy with popularity
  • Assuming democracy eliminates all inequality

Worked Examples

Example 1: Is democracy better for economic development?

Expected answer: Not always faster growth, but more equitable and sustainable development

Solution: Dictatorships like China may grow faster short-term. But democracies distribute benefits more fairly and allow course correction through elections.

Example 2: How does democracy promote dignity?

Expected answer: Constitutional rights for all, especially women, minorities, and lower castes

Solution: Untouchability abolished, equal voting rights, freedom of expression. People can demand respect and justice through legal means.

Practice Questions

EASY Q1: What is the most basic outcome of democracy?
Answer: Accountable and responsive government
EASY Q2: What is the RTI Act?
Answer: Right to Information Act 2005 — citizens can access government records
EASY Q3: Why is democracy considered legitimate?
Answer: Because government is elected by the people and follows their mandate
MED Q4: Why are complaints about democracy a positive sign?
Answer: Shows people are aware, active, and have high expectations — this itself is democratic.
MED Q5: Does democracy guarantee economic equality?
Answer: No. It provides equal voting rights and welfare, but economic disparities can persist.
MED Q6: How does democracy handle conflicts?
Answer: Through negotiation, debate, and rule of law — not by force.
HARD Q7: Compare outcomes of democracy and dictatorship.
Answer: Democracy: slower but accountable, rights protected, dignity ensured. Dictatorship: may grow faster but at cost of freedom, no self-correction.
HARD Q8: Can we say democracy is the best form of government?
Answer: Yes for accountability, rights, dignity. But it's not perfect — corruption, delays exist. Still best because it allows peaceful reform.
HARD Q9: How does transparency make democracy effective?
Answer: RTI, media, judiciary, opposition together ensure government actions are open to scrutiny, reducing corruption.
