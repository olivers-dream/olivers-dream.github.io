Chapter Summary

Examine how gender, religion, and caste influence politics. Study the challenges of communalism, caste-based discrimination, and the fight for gender equality.

  • Gender inequality and feminist movements
  • Communalism, secularism, and religious diversity
  • Caste in politics and politics in caste
  • Reservation policies and social justice

Core Concepts

  • Gender division: women face discrimination in public life, domestic work unpaid, literacy gap exists.
  • Women's political representation: 1/3 reservation in local bodies. In Parliament: ~14% (still low).
  • Feminist movements demand equal wages, legal reforms, anti-harassment laws.
  • Communalism: using religion for political purposes. Leads to stereotyping, division, violence.
  • Secularism in India: no state religion, freedom of religion, ban on discrimination (Articles 25-28).
  • Caste system: historical social hierarchy. Constitution abolishes untouchability (Article 17).
  • Caste in politics: vote banks, reserved constituencies (SC/ST). Politics in caste: caste organizations, caste coalitions.

Key Facts & Articles

  • Women reservation: 1/3 in local bodies (73rd/74th Amendment)
  • Article 17: Abolition of untouchability

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Thinking caste only affects politics (politics also reshapes caste)
  • Confusing secularism with anti-religion
  • Assuming all religious diversity leads to communalism

Worked Examples

Example 1: What is communalism?

Expected answer: Using religion for political mobilisation; belief that one religion is superior

Solution: Leads to stereotyping other religions, demand for separate state, and communal violence. Secularism is the antidote.

Example 2: How has caste entered politics?

Expected answer: Vote banks, caste-based parties, reserved constituencies for SC/ST

Solution: Political parties field candidates from dominant castes. SC/ST have reserved seats. Caste coalitions decide election outcomes.

Practice Questions

EASY Q1: What is the sexual division of labour?
Answer: Women assigned domestic/private roles; men given public/economic roles
EASY Q2: What does secularism mean in India?
Answer: No state religion, freedom of religion, no religious discrimination
EASY Q3: Which Article abolishes untouchability?
Answer: Article 17
MED Q4: Why is women's representation in Parliament low?
Answer: Patriarchal mindset, parties give fewer tickets to women, Women Reservation Bill not yet passed for Parliament.
MED Q5: What is the difference between caste in politics and politics in caste?
Answer: Caste in politics: caste identities used for votes. Politics in caste: politics raises caste consciousness, creates caste alliances.
MED Q6: What is a feminist movement?
Answer: Movement demanding equal rights for women: equal wages, legal reforms, representation.
HARD Q7: Can communalism be ended by law alone?
Answer: No. Needs social awareness, education, inter-community dialogue. Law provides framework but attitude change is key.
HARD Q8: How does overlapping of social differences create possibilities of deep conflict?
Answer: When multiple differences (religion + poverty + region) align, they reinforce each other, creating deeper divisions and greater conflict.
HARD Q9: Evaluate the impact of caste-based reservations.
Answer: Positive: increased representation, social mobility. Criticism: perpetuates caste identity, may not reach poorest. Overall: necessary but insufficient alone.
