Chapter Summary

  • Mendel's laws and Punnett squares
  • Homologous and analogous organs

🗺 Mind Map: Key Concepts

Core Concepts

  • Gene: unit of heredity on chromosomes, controls traits.
  • Dominant: expressed in heterozygous; Recessive: expressed only in homozygous.
  • Mendel's laws: dominance, segregation, independent assortment.
  • Natural selection: survival of fittest drives evolution; evidence from fossils, homologous organs.

Formulas & Equations

  • Punnett square: all possible gamete combinations
  • Monohybrid F2 ratio: 3:1
  • Dihybrid F2 ratio: 9:3:3:1

Worked Examples

Example 1: Heterozygous means?

Expected answer: Two different alleles (e.g. Tt)

Solution: Genotype has both T and t; dominant trait expressed

Example 2: If tall (T) dominant, cross Tt × Tt?

Expected answer: 3 tall : 1 short (3:1)

Solution: TT, Tt, Tt, tt → 3 tall : 1 short

Practice Questions

EASY Q1: Who is the father of genetics?
Answer: Gregor Mendel
EASY Q2: Phenotype is?
Answer: Observable physical traits
EASY Q3: Evidence of evolution?
Answer: Fossils, homologous structures, analogous organs
MED Q4: Monohybrid cross F2 ratio?
Answer: 3:1 (dominant:recessive)
MED Q5: Homozygous example?
Answer: AA or aa (same allele pair)
MED Q6: What are homologous organs?
Answer: Same origin, different function (e.g. arm, wing, flipper)
HARD Q7: AaBb × AaBb dihybrid ratio?
Answer: 9:3:3:1
HARD Q8: Difference between homologous and analogous organs?
Answer: Homologous: same origin; Analogous: same function, different origin
HARD Q9: How does natural selection lead to speciation?
Answer: Geographical isolation + different selections → new species over time
