Core Concepts

  • Carbon: 4 valency, forms strong covalent bonds.
  • Allotropes: diamond, graphite, fullerenes with different structures.
  • Organic compounds: contain carbon, based on C-H, C-C, C-O bonds.
  • Hydrocarbons: alkanes (CₙH₂ₙ₊₂), alkenes (CₙH₂ₙ), alkynes (CₙH₂ₙ₋₂)

Formulas & Equations

  • Alkane: CₙH₂ₙ₊₂
  • Alkene: CₙH₂ₙ
  • CH₄ + 2O₂ → CO₂ + 2H₂O

Worked Examples

Example 1: Write molecular formula of alkane with 5 carbons

Expected answer: C₅H₁₂

Solution: Formula: CₙH₂ₙ₊₂, n=5 → C₅H₁₂

Example 2: Complete combustion: C₂H₆ + O₂ → ?

Expected answer: CO₂ + H₂O

Solution: All C→CO₂, all H→H₂O

Practice Questions

EASY Q1: Valency of carbon?
Answer: 4
EASY Q2: Diamond structure?
Answer: Tetrahedral
EASY Q3: Graphite conducts electricity?
Answer: Yes
MED Q4: C₃H₈ name?
Answer: Propane
MED Q5: Balance: C₂H₆ + O₂ → CO₂ + H₂O
Answer: 2C₂H₆ + 7O₂ → 4CO₂ + 6H₂O
MED Q6: Graphite vs Diamond difference?
Answer: Structure: layers vs tetrahedral
HARD Q7: Write isomers of C₄H₁₀
Answer: Butane and Isobutane (2 isomers)
HARD Q8: Alkene general formula?
Answer: CₙH₂ₙ
HARD Q9: Incomplete combustion produces?
Answer: CO (carbon monoxide) and C (soot)
