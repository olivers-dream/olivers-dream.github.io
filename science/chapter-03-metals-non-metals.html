Core Concepts

  • Metals: lustrous, malleable, ductile, conduct heat/electricity.
  • Metal + O₂ → Metal oxide (basic)
  • Non-metal + O₂ → Non-metal oxide (acidic)
  • Reactivity series: K > Na > Ca > Mg > Al > Zn > Fe > Cu > Au

Formulas & Equations

  • 4Na + O₂ → 2Na₂O
  • Zn + 2HCl → ZnCl₂ + H₂
  • 2Cu + O₂ → 2CuO

  • All metals form only one oxide
  • Forgetting amphoteric compounds
  • Position in reactivity series

Worked Examples

Example 1: Does Zn react with HCl? Write equation.

Expected answer: Zn + 2HCl → ZnCl₂ + H₂

Solution: Zn above H in series, so displaces H₂.

Example 2: More reactive: Mg or Cu?

Expected answer: Mg

Solution: Mg higher in reactivity series than Cu

Practice Questions

EASY Q1: Aluminum: metal or non-metal?
Answer: Metal
EASY Q2: Non-metal that conducts electricity?
Answer: Graphite (carbon)
EASY Q3: Oxygen combines with metal?
Answer: Forms metal oxide
MED Q4: Balance: 2Na + 2H₂O → 2NaOH + H₂
Answer: Correct
MED Q5: Zn reacts with acid. Cu?
Answer: Cu does not react with dilute HCl
MED Q6: Amphoteric oxide example?
Answer: Al₂O₃ or ZnO
HARD Q7: Why Al used for electrical wires?
Answer: Good conductor, lightweight, abundant
HARD Q8: Passivation of iron?
Answer: Forms protective oxide layer
HARD Q9: Write: Cu + O₂ balanced
Answer: 2Cu + O₂ → 2CuO
