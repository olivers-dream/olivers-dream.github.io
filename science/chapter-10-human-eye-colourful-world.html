Chapter Summary

Study the structure and functioning of the human eye, defects of vision and their correction, refraction through prism, dispersion of white light, and atmospheric refraction phenomena.

  • Human eye structure and image formation
  • Defects of vision: myopia, hypermetropia, presbyopia
  • Refraction through prism and dispersion (VIBGYOR)
  • Atmospheric refraction and scattering of light

Core Concepts

  • Human eye: cornea, iris, pupil, lens, retina work together to form images.
  • Defects: Myopia (near-sightedness) corrected by concave lens, Hypermetropia by convex lens.
  • Presbyopia: loss of accommodation with age, corrected using bifocal lens.
  • Dispersion: white light splits into 7 colours (VIBGYOR) through a prism.

Formulas & Equations

  • Power of lens P = 1/f (dioptre)
  • Near point: 25 cm (normal eye)
  • Far point: infinity (normal eye)

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Confusing myopia and hypermetropia corrections
  • Thinking scattering and dispersion are the same
  • Forgetting that eye lens is a converging lens

Worked Examples

Example 1: A person cannot see objects beyond 5m. What defect and correction?

Expected answer: Myopia; concave lens of focal length -5m

Solution: Far point = 5m instead of ∞, so myopia. Concave lens f = -5m brings ∞ to 5m.

Example 2: Why does the sky appear blue?

Expected answer: Scattering of shorter wavelengths (blue) by atmosphere

Solution: Blue light scattered most (shorter λ) by atmospheric particles — Rayleigh scattering.

Practice Questions

EASY Q1: What controls the amount of light entering the eye?
Answer: Iris/Pupil
EASY Q2: What is the least distance of distinct vision?
Answer: 25 cm
EASY Q3: VIBGYOR stands for?
Answer: Violet, Indigo, Blue, Green, Yellow, Orange, Red
MED Q4: Why do stars twinkle?
Answer: Atmospheric refraction — changing refractive index
MED Q5: What type of lens corrects hypermetropia?
Answer: Convex lens
MED Q6: What is dispersion of light?
Answer: Splitting of white light into 7 colours by prism
HARD Q7: Why is the sun reddish at sunrise/sunset?
Answer: Blue scattered away, red/orange least scattered reaches eye
HARD Q8: A person needs +2D lens. What defect and focal length?
Answer: Hypermetropia; f = +50 cm
HARD Q9: Explain why danger signals are red
Answer: Red scattered least, travels farthest through atmosphere
