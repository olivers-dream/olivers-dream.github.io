Core Concepts

  • Current: flow of charge (I = Q/t), measured in Amperes.
  • Voltage: potential difference, measured in Volts.
  • Resistance: opposition to flow (R = ρL/A), depends on material, length, area.
  • Ohm's Law: V = IR (at constant temperature).

Formulas & Equations

  • Ohm's Law: V = IR
  • Power: P = VI = I²R = V²/R
  • Energy: E = Pt
  • Resistance: R = ρL/A

Worked Examples

Example 1: Resistances 4Ω, 6Ω in series: total?

Expected answer: 10Ω

Solution: R_total = 4 + 6 = 10Ω

Example 2: 4Ω, 6Ω in parallel: total?

Expected answer: 2.4Ω

Solution: 1/R = 1/4 + 1/6 = 5/12, R = 2.4Ω

Practice Questions

EASY Q1: SI unit of current?
Answer: Ampere (A)
EASY Q2: What does ammeter measure?
Answer: Current
EASY Q3: Domestic voltage in India?
Answer: 220V, 50Hz
MED Q4: Resistivity depends on?
Answer: Material and temperature (not length/area)
MED Q5: Fuse wire should have?
Answer: Low melting point and high resistivity
MED Q6: Why series circuit not used for household wiring?
Answer: If one device fails, all stop; unequal voltage
HARD Q7: 100W bulb for 10 hours, electricity cost at ₹5/kWh?
Answer: 1 kWh × ₹5 = ₹5
HARD Q8: Two 4Ω resistors in parallel, then in series with 3Ω. Total R?
Answer: 2 + 3 = 5Ω
HARD Q9: Why does a filament bulb glow but connecting wires do not?
Answer: Filament has high resistance, wires have negligible resistance
