🗺 Mind Map: Key Concepts

Central concept connects to main topics.

Core Concepts

  • Law of reflection: angle of incidence = angle of reflection.
  • Concave mirror: converging, used for real images; Convex mirror: diverging, wide view.
  • Refraction: bending of light at medium boundary (Snell's law).
  • Lens formula: 1/v − 1/u = 1/f; Power P = 1/f (dioptre).

Formulas & Equations

  • Mirror formula: 1/v + 1/u = 1/f
  • Magnification: m = -v/u
  • Lens power: P = 1/f (D)
  • Snell's law: n₁ sin i = n₂ sin r

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Confusing sign convention for mirrors vs lenses
  • Focal length always positive
  • Real image always bigger

Worked Examples

Example 1: Object at 2f in concave mirror, image?

Expected answer: At 2f (real, inverted, same size)

Solution: u = 2f → v = 2f, magnification = -1

Example 2: Refractive index of glass is 1.5. Speed of light in glass?

Expected answer: 2 × 10⁸ m/s

Solution: v = c/n = 3×10⁸/1.5 = 2×10⁸ m/s

Practice Questions

EASY Q1: Angle of incidence = ?
Answer: Angle of reflection
EASY Q2: Convex mirror used for?
Answer: Rear-view mirror (wide field of view)
EASY Q3: Power of lens formula?
Answer: P = 1/f (dioptre, f in metres)
MED Q4: Focal length: f = R/?
Answer: f = R/2
MED Q5: Real image is always?
Answer: Inverted
MED Q6: Converging lens: convex or concave?
Answer: Convex
HARD Q7: Object at u = -30cm, f = -10cm (concave mirror), find v
Answer: v = -15 cm (real, inverted)
HARD Q8: 2 thin lenses in contact: net power?
Answer: P = P₁ + P₂
HARD Q9: Why does a pencil appear bent in water?
Answer: Refraction: light bends at water-air boundary
