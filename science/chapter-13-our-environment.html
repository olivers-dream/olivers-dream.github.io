Core Concepts

  • Ecosystem: biotic (living) and abiotic (non-living) components interact.
  • Food chain: producers → primary consumers → secondary consumers → decomposers.
  • Only 10% energy transfers between trophic levels (10% rule).
  • Ozone layer depletion by CFCs; biodegradable vs non-biodegradable waste.

Formulas & Equations

  • Energy at nth level = 10^(n-1) % of producer energy
  • 10% law: only 10% energy passes to next trophic level

  • Confusing food chain with food web

Worked Examples

Example 1: If producers have 10000 J, energy at third trophic level?

Expected answer: 100 J

Solution: Producer 10000 → Herbivore 1000 → Carnivore 100 (10% each step)

Example 2: Why are food chains short (3-4 levels)?

Expected answer: Energy decreases at each level (10% rule) — too little energy remains

Solution: Only 10% transfers → after 4 levels barely any energy left

Practice Questions

EASY Q1: Producers in a food chain are?
Answer: Green plants (autotrophs)
EASY Q2: What are decomposers?
Answer: Organisms that break down dead matter (bacteria, fungi)
EASY Q3: Ozone layer protects from?
Answer: Ultraviolet (UV) radiation
MED Q4: What is biological magnification?
Answer: Increase in concentration of toxins at higher trophic levels
MED Q5: Give example of a food chain
Answer: Grass → Grasshopper → Frog → Snake → Eagle
MED Q6: Difference: biodegradable vs non-biodegradable?
Answer: Biodegradable decomposes naturally; non-biodegradable does not
HARD Q7: Why is ozone layer thinning?
Answer: CFCs (chlorofluorocarbons) break ozone into O₂
HARD Q8: What is the role of decomposers in ecosystem?
Answer: Recycle nutrients back to soil, essential for nutrient cycling
HARD Q9: If 100000 kJ at producer level, energy at top carnivore (4th level)?
Answer: 100 kJ (10% × 10% × 10%)
