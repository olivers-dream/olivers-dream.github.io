Core Concepts

  • Acid: pH < 7, sour taste, turns blue litmus red.
  • Base: pH > 7, bitter taste, turns red litmus blue.
  • pH scale 0-14: below 7 acidic, above 7 basic, 7 neutral.
  • Neutralization: Acid + Base → Salt + Water

Formulas & Equations

  • pH = -log₁₀[H⁺]
  • Acid + Base → Salt + Water
  • HCl + NaOH → NaCl + H₂O

Worked Examples

Example 1: pH of pure water?

Expected answer: 7

Solution: [H⁺]=[OH⁻]=10⁻⁷, so pH = 7 (neutral)

Example 2: Products when acid + base?

Expected answer: Salt and water

Solution: Neutralization always gives salt + water

Practice Questions

EASY Q1: Solution with pH 3 is?
Answer: Acidic
EASY Q2: Blue litmus in HCl?
Answer: Turns red
EASY Q3: Method to test acid/base?
Answer: Litmus paper or pH paper
MED Q4: pH of 0.01 M HCl?
Answer: pH = 2
MED Q5: Balance: H₂SO₄ + NaOH→Na₂SO₄+H₂O
Answer: H₂SO₄ + 2NaOH → Na₂SO₄ + 2H₂O
MED Q6: NaCl acidic or neutral?
Answer: Neutral
HARD Q7: NH₄Cl acidic or basic? Why?
Answer: Acidic: NH₄⁺ hydrolyzes releasing H⁺
HARD Q8: pH of 0.1 M NaOH?
Answer: pH = 13
HARD Q9: Write: Al(OH)₃ + HCl balanced
Answer: Al(OH)₃ + 3HCl → AlCl₃ + 3H₂O
