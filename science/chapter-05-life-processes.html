Chapter Summary

Study vital life processes: nutrition, respiration, transport, excretion. Learn autotrophic vs heterotrophic nutrition and photosynthesis.

  • Types of nutrition: autotrophic and heterotrophic
  • Photosynthesis and cellular respiration
  • Transport in plants (xylem/phloem) and animals (blood)
  • Excretion: kidneys, nephrons, urine formation

Core Concepts

  • Nutrition: taking in and processing food for energy and building.
  • Photosynthesis: 6CO₂ + 6H₂O → C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂ (autotrophic nutrition).
  • Cellular respiration: aerobic uses O₂, anaerobic without O₂.
  • Transport: blood circulation in animals, xylem/phloem in plants.

Formulas & Equations

  • Photosynthesis: 6CO₂ + 6H₂O → C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂
  • Aerobic: C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂ → 6CO₂ + 6H₂O + energy

Worked Examples

Example 1: What is produced in photosynthesis?

Expected answer: Glucose and Oxygen

Solution: Plants use CO₂+H₂O+light

Example 2: Aerobic respiration needs?

Expected answer: Oxygen

Solution: With oxygen: full breakdown of glucose

Practice Questions

EASY Q1: Plants are autotrophs or heterotrophs?
Answer: Autotrophs
EASY Q2: Respiration produces energy as?
Answer: ATP
EASY Q3: Oxygen produced in which process?
Answer: Photosynthesis
MED Q4: Anaerobic respiration in muscles produces?
Answer: Lactic acid (causes fatigue)
MED Q5: In photosynthesis, CO₂ is reduced to?
Answer: Glucose
MED Q6: Transport in plants via?
Answer: Xylem (water up) and Phloem (food down)
HARD Q7: Fermentation: yeast vs muscle?
Answer: Yeast: ethanol+CO₂, Muscle: lactic acid
HARD Q8: What are the components of blood?
Answer: Plasma, RBC, WBC, platelets
HARD Q9: Why plants respire at night?
Answer: No light for photosynthesis, still need ATP
