Chapter Summary

Study how organisms reproduce through asexual and sexual methods. Learn about fission, budding, vegetative propagation, pollination, fertilization and human reproductive system.

  • Asexual reproduction: fission, budding, vegetative propagation
  • Sexual reproduction: gametes, pollination, fertilization
  • Human reproductive system and puberty
  • Reproductive health and contraception

Core Concepts

  • Reproduction ensures continuation of species and passes genetic information.
  • Asexual: single parent, identical offspring — fission, budding, fragmentation, vegetative propagation.
  • Sexual: two parents, genetic variation — involves gametes, fertilization, zygote formation.
  • Human reproductive system: male (testes, sperm), female (ovaries, eggs, uterus).

Formulas & Equations

  • DNA copying → variation + inheritance
  • Pollination → Fertilization → Seed → Fruit
  • Menstrual cycle ≈ 28 days

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Confusing vegetative propagation with regeneration
  • Thinking asexual reproduction has no variation at all
  • Mixing up pollination and fertilization

Worked Examples

Example 1: Name two methods of asexual reproduction in unicellular organisms

Expected answer: Binary fission and budding

Solution: Amoeba uses binary fission, Yeast uses budding — both produce from single parent

Example 2: What happens during fertilization in humans?

Expected answer: Sperm fuses with egg to form zygote

Solution: Sperm (n) + Ovum (n) → Zygote (2n) which develops into embryo

Practice Questions

EASY Q1: Amoeba reproduces by?
Answer: Binary fission
EASY Q2: What is vegetative propagation?
Answer: New plants from vegetative parts like stem, root, leaf
EASY Q3: Male reproductive cell in humans?
Answer: Sperm
MED Q4: Why is variation beneficial?
Answer: Helps species survive changing environments
MED Q5: What is the role of placenta?
Answer: Provides nutrients and oxygen to embryo from mother
MED Q6: Difference between self and cross pollination?
Answer: Self: same flower; Cross: different flowers
HARD Q7: Why is DNA copying essential in reproduction?
Answer: Passes genetic information; errors create variation for evolution
HARD Q8: How does the embryo get nourishment in humans?
Answer: Through placenta embedded in uterus wall
HARD Q9: Explain why vegetative propagation is used by farmers
Answer: Produces genetically identical plants with desired traits quickly
