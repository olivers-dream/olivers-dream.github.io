Chapter Context (Detailed Breakdown)

This chapter explains how two linear equations can have one, none, or infinitely many solutions. You master elimination, substitution, cross-multiplication and graph interpretation.

  • Represent each equation as a line and read solution type from line behavior.
  • Use consistency ratios to decide one/no/infinite solution before solving.
  • Master substitution, elimination, and cross multiplication method selection.
  • Convert word problems into two variables and validate final ordered pair.

Remember Before Solving

  • Check ratio conditions before solving.
  • Keep variable meaning fixed in word problems.
  • Verify in both equations.

Boxed Items (Must-Memorize)

  • Consistency Conditions
  • Elimination Method
  • Graphical Interpretation

Section A - NCERT Style Practice

Solve in notebook first. Use reveal only to verify method.

A1. Solve x + y = 9 and x - y = 1.

Expected final answer: x=5,y=4

Steps: Add equations: 2x=10 so x=5. Put in x+y=9 gives y=4.

A2. 2x+3y=7 and 4x+6y=14 have how many solutions?

Expected final answer: Infinite

Steps: Second equation is exactly 2× first; both represent same line ⇒ infinitely many solutions.

Section B - Mixed Difficulty Drill

EASY B1. Solve 3x+y=11 and x+y=7.

Answer: x=2,y=5.

EASY B2. Do x+y=5 and 2x+2y=10 have unique solution?

Answer: No, infinitely many

EASY B3. Are x+y=1 and 2x+2y=3 consistent?

Answer: No, no solution

MED B4. Find k for infinite solutions: 2x+3y=5 and 4x+6y=k.

Answer: k=10

MED B5. Solve by elimination: 3x+2y=17 and 2x+3y=18.

Answer: x=3,y=4

MED B6. Using graph method concept, describe three cases of solution type.

Answer: Unique (intersecting), None (parallel), Infinite (same line)

HARD B7. Boat speed problem downstream/upstream.

Answer: Set two equations in still-water and stream speeds and solve.

HARD B8. For 2x+ay=7 and 4x+2y=14 to have infinite solutions, find a.

Answer: a=1

HARD B9. Age problem: Father is 3 times son's age, in 5 years only 2.5 times. Ages?

Answer: Father=45, Son=15

Quick Formula + Error Revision

  • a1/a2 ≠ b1/b2 ⇒ unique solution
  • a1/a2 = b1/b2 ≠ c1/c2 ⇒ no solution
  • a1/a2 = b1/b2 = c1/c2 ⇒ infinitely many solutions

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Wrong sign while moving terms across equals.
  • Forgetting to check consistency condition.
