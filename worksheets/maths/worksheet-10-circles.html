Chapter Context (Detailed Breakdown)

This theorem-based chapter focuses on tangents and circle properties used in proofs and constructions.

  • Differentiate tangent, secant, and chord clearly.
  • Apply tangent-radius perpendicular property in proofs.
  • Use equal tangent theorem from external point repeatedly.
  • Structure geometry proof with marked equal radii and right angles.

Remember Before Solving

  • Tangent touches at one point only.
  • Radius at contact is always perpendicular.
  • Tangents from same external point are equal.

Boxed Items (Must-Memorize)

  • Tangent-Radius Perpendicular
  • Equal Tangents Theorem
  • Circle Proof Markings

Section A - NCERT Style Practice

Solve in notebook first. Use reveal only to verify method.

A1. Angle between radius and tangent at contact point?

Expected final answer: 90

Steps: By circle theorem, tangent at any point is perpendicular to radius through that point.

A2. From external point P, tangents PA and PB are drawn. Relation?

Expected final answer: PA=PB

Steps: Using congruent triangles OPA and OPB (RHS), PA = PB.

Section B - Mixed Difficulty Drill

EASY B1. Tangents from point on circle?

Answer: One tangent

EASY B2. Can a line intersect circle at 3 points?

Answer: No, maximum 2 points.

EASY B3. Angle between radius and tangent at contact point?

Answer: 90°

MED B4. If one tangent length is 9 cm, other?

Answer: 9 cm

MED B5. From external point P, 2 tangents to circle. What do you call this point?

Answer: External point / Outside the circle

MED B6. If PA and PB are tangents to circle with center O, what is angle OPA + angle OBP?

Answer: 90°

HARD B7. Proof idea for equal tangents.

Answer: Use radii and RHS congruence.

HARD B8. Tangent length from (12, 5) to circle x²+y²=100 is?

Answer: 13

HARD B9. Two circles with tangent length 15 cm between them. If radii are 12 and 5, find distance between centers.

Answer: 20 cm (external) or 2 cm (internal)

Quick Formula + Error Revision

  • PA = PB (from same external point P to contact points A,B)

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Treating secant as tangent.
  • Not marking right angle at point of contact.
