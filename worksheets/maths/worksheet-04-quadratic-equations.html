Chapter Context (Detailed Breakdown)

Quadratic equations model area, motion and product-based problems. You solve by factorization, completing square and formula, and use discriminant to classify roots.

  • Identify quadratic form and rearrange equations into ax²+bx+c=0 properly.
  • Solve by factorization where possible and formula method for all cases.
  • Use discriminant to predict root nature before solving.
  • Apply quadratics in geometry, area, and consecutive number problems.

Remember Before Solving

  • Write in standard form first.
  • Don't drop ± in formula.
  • Discriminant first saves time.

Boxed Items (Must-Memorize)

  • Quadratic Formula
  • Discriminant D=b²-4ac
  • Nature of Roots Table

Section A - NCERT Style Practice

Solve in notebook first. Use reveal only to verify method.

A1. Solve x² - 7x + 12 = 0.

Expected final answer: 3,4

Steps: (x-3)(x-4)=0, so x=3 or x=4.

A2. For 2x² + 4x + 5 = 0, what is nature of roots?

Expected final answer: No real roots

Steps: D = 4² - 4·2·5 = -24 < 0, so no real roots.

Section B - Mixed Difficulty Drill

EASY B1. Solve x²-9x+20=0.

Answer: x=4,5

EASY B2. Is 5x²-10x+15=0 solvable in reals? Why?

Answer: No, D<0

EASY B3. Solve x²=49.

Answer: x=±7

MED B4. Solve 3x²-5x-2=0.

Answer: x=2 and x=-1/3

MED B5. For x²+5x+k=0, what k gives equal roots?

Answer: k=25/4

MED B6. Nature of roots of x² - 2x + 1 = 0.

Answer: Equal real roots (D=0)

HARD B7. Product of two consecutive positives is 306.

Answer: 17 and 18

HARD B8. Solve (x+1)²-(x²-1)=0.

Answer: x=-1

HARD B9. Area of rectangle is 120, perimeter is 46. Find dimensions.

Answer: 12×10

Quick Formula + Error Revision

  • x = [-b ± √(b²−4ac)] / 2a
  • D = b²−4ac

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Dropping ± in quadratic formula.
  • Errors while squaring negatives in D.
