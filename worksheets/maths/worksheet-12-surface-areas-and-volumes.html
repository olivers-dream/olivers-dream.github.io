Chapter Context (Detailed Breakdown)

Covers TSA/CSA and volume formulas for 3D solids and combinations. Strong formula memory + unit consistency needed.

  • Separate CSA, TSA, and Volume conceptually for every solid.
  • Memorize cylinder, cone, sphere, hemisphere formulas with units.
  • Convert all dimensions to same unit before substitution.
  • Break composite solids into known parts and combine results logically.

Remember Before Solving

  • CSA and TSA are different.
  • Volume uses cubic units.
  • Cone volume has 1/3 factor.

Section A - NCERT Style Practice

Solve in notebook first. Use reveal only to verify method.

A1. Volume of cylinder r=3 cm, h=10 cm.

Expected final answer: 90π

Steps: V = πr²h = π×9×10 = 90π cm³.

A2. CSA of cone r=7 cm, l=25 cm.

Expected final answer: 175π

Steps: CSA = πrl = π×7×25 = 175π cm².

Quick Formula + Error Revision

  • Cylinder volume = πr²h
  • Cone CSA = πrl
  • Sphere volume = 4/3 πr³

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Mixing radius and diameter values.
  • Using slant height in volume formulas.
