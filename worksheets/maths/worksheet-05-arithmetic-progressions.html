Chapter Context (Detailed Breakdown)

You learn sequence patterns with constant difference, nth term, and sum formulas. This chapter appears in direct and word-problem forms in board exams.

  • Identify arithmetic progression from constant difference patterns.
  • Use nth-term formula for direct term-position questions.
  • Use sum formula to compute large totals without manual addition.
  • Model day-to-day contexts (savings, seating, pattern growth) using AP.

Remember Before Solving

  • Use (n-1)d, not nd.
  • d must be constant.
  • Use Sn formula for long sums.

Boxed Items (Must-Memorize)

  • nth Term Formula
  • Sum of n Terms
  • AP Identification Rule

Section A - NCERT Style Practice

Solve in notebook first. Use reveal only to verify method.

A1. Find 20th term of AP 3, 7, 11, ...

Expected final answer: 79

Steps: a=3, d=4. a20 = 3 + 19×4 = 79.

A2. Find S15 for AP 2,5,8,...

Expected final answer: 345

Steps: S15 = 15/2[2×2 + 14×3] = 15/2[46] = 345.

Section B - Mixed Difficulty Drill

EASY B1. Find d: 9,14,19,...

Answer: 5

EASY B2. Is 100,101,102,... an AP? If yes, d=?

Answer: Yes, d=1

EASY B3. First term a=2, d=3. Find 5th term.

Answer: 14

MED B4. Which term of 5,9,13,... is 53?

Answer: 13th term

MED B5. Sum of first 10 natural numbers.

Answer: 55

MED B6. AP has a=7, l=49, n=10. Find S.

Answer: S=280

HARD B7. Find n if S_n of 7,13,19,... is 468.

Answer: n=12

HARD B8. Find AP where sum of m and n terms are in ratio m²:n².

Answer: a=d (forms AP with ratio)

HARD B9. Arithmetic progression has 3 consecutive terms summing to 15 with product 80. Find AP:

Answer: 2,5,8

Quick Formula + Error Revision

  • a_n = a + (n−1)d
  • S_n = n/2[2a + (n−1)d]

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Using n instead of n−1 in nth term.
  • Mixing up nth term and sum formulas.
