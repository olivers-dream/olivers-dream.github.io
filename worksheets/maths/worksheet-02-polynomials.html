Chapter Context (Detailed Breakdown)

This chapter teaches polynomial structure, degree, zeros, graph meaning, and relation between roots and coefficients for quadratics. It helps in equation-solving and function thinking.

  • Build polynomial basics: term, coefficient, degree, and standard forms.
  • Interpret zeros algebraically (p(x)=0) and graphically (x-axis intersections).
  • Use root-coefficient relations for quadratics to avoid lengthy factorization.
  • Apply division algorithm and factor theorem in proof and objective questions.

Remember Before Solving

  • Zero means p(x)=0 exactly.
  • Sign in α+β is negative.
  • Check factors using substitution.

Boxed Items (Must-Memorize)

  • Zeros of Polynomial
  • Remainder Theorem
  • Factor Theorem

Section A - NCERT Style Practice

Solve in notebook first. Use reveal only to verify method.

A1. Find zeros of x² - 5x + 6.

Expected final answer: 2,3

Steps: Factor: x² - 5x + 6 = (x-2)(x-3). So zeros are 2 and 3.

A2. For 2x² + 7x + 3, find α+β.

Expected final answer: -7/2

Steps: For ax²+bx+c, α+β = -b/a = -7/2.

Section B - Mixed Difficulty Drill

EASY B1. Find zeros of x² - 9.

Answer: x = ±3.

EASY B2. Evaluate p(1) for p(x)=x²+2x.

Answer: 3

EASY B3. Degree of polynomial 5x³+2x+1?

Answer: 3

MED B4. If zeros are 4 and -2, form polynomial.

Answer: x² -2x -8

MED B5. Factor x² - 7x + 10.

Answer: (x-2)(x-5)

MED B6. Find relationship between roots and coefficients for 3x²-9x+6.

Answer: Sum=-3, product=2

HARD B7. Divide 2x³+3x²-11x-6 by (x-2).

Answer: Quotient 2x²+7x+3, remainder 0.

HARD B8. For x²+px+q with roots α and 2α, find p in terms of α.

Answer: p = -3α

HARD B9. If p(x)=x⁴-1, find all zeros.

Answer: ±1

Quick Formula + Error Revision

  • α + β = -b/a
  • αβ = c/a

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Confusing factor with zero (related but not same wording).
  • Sign errors in α+β relation.
