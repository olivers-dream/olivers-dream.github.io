Chapter Context (Detailed Breakdown)

Extends circle geometry into area and perimeter of sectors and segments. Important for mensuration and application sums.

  • Revise circle area and circumference as foundation.
  • Compute arc length and sector area using angle fraction.
  • Find segment area by subtracting triangle from sector.
  • Handle ring (annulus) and mixed-figure mensuration carefully.

Remember Before Solving

  • Sector uses angle fraction θ/360.
  • Segment = sector - triangle.
  • Keep units consistent for area.

Boxed Items (Must-Memorize)

  • Arc Length Formula
  • Sector Area Formula
  • Segment Area Relation

Section A - NCERT Style Practice

Solve in notebook first. Use reveal only to verify method.

A1. Area of circle radius 7 cm (π=22/7).

Expected final answer: 154

Steps: Area = πr² = (22/7)×49 = 154 cm².

A2. Area of 90° sector radius 14 cm.

Expected final answer: 154

Steps: (90/360)×π×14² = 1/4×π×196 = 49π = 154 cm².

Quick Formula + Error Revision

  • Area circle = πr²
  • Circumference = 2πr
  • Area sector = (θ/360)πr²

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Using degree in place of fraction wrongly.
  • Forgetting subtraction for segment/ring.
