Chapter Context (Detailed Breakdown)

Applies trigonometric ratios to practical measurements like heights of towers and distances using angles of elevation/depression.

  • Translate elevation/depression text into accurate right-triangle diagrams.
  • Select tan/sin/cos based on known and unknown sides.
  • Solve one-step and two-step height-distance applications.
  • Check units and interpretation of final practical answer.

Remember Before Solving

  • Draw diagram before equation.
  • Elevation is above horizontal.
  • Choose tan when height-base pair appears.

Boxed Items (Must-Memorize)

  • Angle of Elevation/Depression
  • Height-Distance Setup
  • Diagram-first Rule

Section A - NCERT Style Practice

Solve in notebook first. Use reveal only to verify method.

A1. From 20 m away, angle elevation is 45°. Height?

Expected final answer: 20

Steps: tan45 = h/20 = 1 ⇒ h=20 m.

A2. Kite string 60 m at 30° elevation. Height?

Expected final answer: 30

Steps: h = 60 sin30 = 60×1/2 = 30 m.

Section B - Mixed Difficulty Drill

EASY B1. Define angle of elevation.

Answer: Angle above horizontal to object.

EASY B2. At distance 15 m, angle elevation is 45°. Height?

Answer: 15 m

EASY B3. What is angle of depression?

Answer: Angle below horizontal when looking down.

MED B4. Pole shadow 10 m, sun 60°. Height?

Answer: 10√3 m

MED B5. Ladder of length 13 m leans on wall with base 5 m away. Height reached?

Answer: 12 m

MED B6. From top of 20 m high building, angle depression to ground object is 30°. Distance from building?

Answer: 20√3 m

HARD B7. Two points 50 m apart observe top at 30°,60°.

Answer: Height = 25√3 m

HARD B8. Kite string 100 m at 45° elevation. Horizontal and vertical distances?

Answer: Both = 50√2 m

HARD B9. Two buildings: From top of first (50m), angle depression to base of second is 30°. From top of second (h), angle elevation to first top is 45°. Find h.

Answer: h = 50(√3-1) m

Quick Formula + Error Revision

  • tanθ = height/base
  • sinθ = height/hypotenuse
  • cosθ = base/hypotenuse

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Skipping diagram leads to wrong sides.
  • Confusing angle of depression with elevation.
