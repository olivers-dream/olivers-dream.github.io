Chapter Context (Detailed Breakdown)

This chapter maps algebra to geometry. You compute distances, divide line segments in ratios, and find midpoint coordinates.

  • Read and plot points confidently on Cartesian plane.
  • Compute distances and midpoints with correct coordinate order.
  • Use section formula for ratio division of line segments.
  • Apply coordinate methods to test collinearity and simple geometric proofs.

Remember Before Solving

  • Coordinate order is (x,y).
  • Use brackets while subtracting negatives.
  • Section ratio order matters.

A1. Distance between (2,3) and (8,11).

Expected final answer: 10

Steps: √[(8−2)² + (11−3)²] = √(36+64)=10.

A2. Midpoint of (1,5) and (7,9).

Quick Formula + Error Revision

  • Distance = √[(x2−x1)² + (y2−y1)²]
  • Midpoint = ((x1+x2)/2, (y1+y2)/2)
  • Section: ((mx2+nx1)/(m+n), (my2+ny1)/(m+n))

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Swapping x and y in formulas.
  • Wrong ratio order in section formula.
