Chapter Context (Detailed Breakdown)

Probability quantifies chance from 0 to 1. You use favorable outcomes / total outcomes for equally likely events.

  • Define experiment, outcome, event, and sample space clearly.
  • Apply favorable/total rule only for equally likely outcomes.
  • Use complement relation P(E)+P(not E)=1 for shortcuts.
  • Practice dice, cards, and coin events with careful counting.

Remember Before Solving

  • Probability range is 0 to 1.
  • Use equally likely outcomes rule carefully.
  • P(E)+P(not E)=1 shortcut.

Boxed Items (Must-Memorize)

  • Theoretical Probability
  • Complement Rule
  • Event and Sample Space

Section A - NCERT Style Practice

Solve in notebook first. Use reveal only to verify method.

A1. Probability of head on fair coin?

Expected final answer: 1/2

Steps: Favorable outcomes = 1 (H), total = 2 (H,T). So P(H)=1/2.

A2. Probability of prime on die?

Expected final answer: 1/2

Steps: Primes on die: 2,3,5 (3 outcomes), total 6, so 3/6=1/2.

Section B - Mixed Difficulty Drill

EASY B1. P(even number on die)?

Answer: 1/2

EASY B2. P(rolling 1 on die)?

Answer: 1/6

EASY B3. Can probability be 1.5?

Answer: No, always 0 ≤ P(E) ≤ 1

MED B4. Bag with 5R,3B,2G. P(blue)?

Answer: 3/10

MED B5. Cards 1 to 20. P(multiple of 5)?

Answer: 1/5

MED B6. Outcomes when 2 coins are tossed.

Answer: HH, HT, TH, TT (4 outcomes)

HARD B7. Two coins. P(at least one head)?

Answer: 3/4

HARD B8. Card from 52. P(red king or queen)?

Answer: 4/52 = 1/13

HARD B9. Die + coin together. Total equally likely outcomes.

Answer: 12 (6 die × 2 coin outcomes)

Quick Formula + Error Revision

  • P(E) = Number of favorable outcomes / Total outcomes

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Not checking if outcomes are equally likely.
  • Counting favorable outcomes incorrectly.
