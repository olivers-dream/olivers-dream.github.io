Chapter Context (Detailed Breakdown)

This chapter builds the base of number theory: Euclid algorithm, Fundamental Theorem of Arithmetic, and decimal expansion of rationals. You learn how to find HCF/LCM fast, and how prime factor patterns decide terminating or recurring decimals.

  • Start with Euclid Division Lemma and understand how repeated division gives HCF quickly.
  • Study prime factorization and why each composite number has a unique prime breakdown.
  • Connect prime factors to HCF/LCM relations used in direct board sums.
  • Classify decimals as terminating or recurring using denominator factors in lowest form.

Remember Before Solving

  • Always reduce fraction before termination test.
  • Euclid stops only at remainder 0.
  • Prime factors decide decimal behavior.

Boxed Items (Must-Memorize)

  • Euclid Division Lemma
  • Fundamental Theorem of Arithmetic
  • Terminating Decimal Criterion

Section A - NCERT Style Practice

Solve in notebook first. Use reveal only to verify method.

A1. Use Euclid's algorithm to find HCF(1071, 462).

Expected final answer: 21

Steps: 1071 = 462 × 2 + 147 | 462 = 147 × 3 + 21 | 147 = 21 × 7 + 0 | ∴ HCF = 21

A2. Will 7^n ever end in 0 for any natural n?

Expected final answer: No

Steps: Ending in 0 means number has factor 10 = 2×5. But 7^n has only factor 7, so impossible.

Section B - Mixed Difficulty Drill

EASY B1. Find HCF of 135 and 225.

Answer: 45

EASY B2. Find the HCF of 48 and 64.

Answer: 16

EASY B3. Is 1.23 terminating or recurring?

Answer: Terminating

MED B4. Show that √5 is irrational.

Answer: Assume √5 = p/q in lowest form. Then 5 divides p and q, contradiction.

MED B5. Find LCM of 12 and 18 using HCF.

Answer: 36

MED B6. Will 2^n × 5^m × 7 ever give a terminating decimal?

Answer: No, because 7 is a prime factor.

HARD B7. Find LCM of 306 and 657.

Answer: HCF=9, so LCM=(306×657)/9 = 22338.

HARD B8. Prove that √2 + √3 is irrational.

Answer: Assume rational, square both sides, derive contradiction.

HARD B9. Find HCF and LCM of 117 and 221.

Answer: HCF=13, LCM=1989

Quick Formula + Error Revision

  • LCM × HCF = Product of two numbers
  • For p/q in lowest form: terminating decimal iff q = 2^m 5^n

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Not reducing fraction before checking denominator factors.
  • Stopping Euclid steps too early before remainder becomes 0.
