Chapter Context (Detailed Breakdown)

Section A - NCERT Style Practice

Solve in notebook first. Use reveal only to verify method.

A1. If AB/PQ = 2/3 for similar triangles, find area ratio.

Expected final answer: 4/9

Steps: Area ratio = (AB/PQ)² = (2/3)² = 4/9.

A2. Right triangle with legs 6 and 8. Hypotenuse?

Expected final answer: 10

Steps: By Pythagoras, c=√(6²+8²)=√100=10.

Section B - Mixed Difficulty Drill

EASY B1. Name one similarity criterion.

Answer: AA or SAS or SSS

EASY B2. Are all equilateral triangles similar?

Answer: Yes

EASY B3. If side ratio of 2 similar triangles is 3:4, area ratio?

Answer: 9:16

MED B4. If perimeter ratio is 2:3, side ratio?

Answer: 2:3

MED B5. Angle bisector theorem: definition/statement.

Answer: Bisector divides opposite side in ratio of adjacent sides.

MED B6. Basic proportionality theorem (Thales) statement.

Answer: Line parallel to one side divides other two sides proportionally.

HARD B7. Two triangles have angles 50°,60°,70° and 70°,50°,60°. Similar?

Answer: Yes (AA criterion)

HARD B8. Angle bisector theorem proof idea.

Answer: Use triangle similarity and proportionality.

HARD B9. If triangles ABC and DEF have AB/DE=BC/EF=5/7, but ∠B≠∠E. How are they related?

Answer: Not necessarily similar (need one angle equal)

Quick Formula + Error Revision

  • If Δ1~Δ2 and side ratio = k, then area ratio = k²

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Matching wrong corresponding vertices.
  • Forgetting to square ratio for areas.
