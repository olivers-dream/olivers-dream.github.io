Practice Exercises

EASY Q1: What does "repulsive" mean?
Answer: Causing strong dislike or disgust.
EASY Q2: Change to indirect: She said, "I want to go to town."
Answer: She said that she wanted to go to town.
EASY Q3: Make exclamatory: "The bus ride was amazing."
Answer: What an amazing bus ride it was! / How amazing the bus ride was!
MED Q4: Change to indirect: The conductor asked, "Where are you going, little girl?"
Answer: The conductor asked the little girl where she was going.
MED Q5: Change to indirect: Valli said, "I have already paid my fare."
Answer: Valli said that she had already paid her fare.
MED Q6: Write a 3-sentence biographical sketch opening for Valli.
Answer: Valliammai was an eight-year-old girl from a small village in Tamil Nadu. Despite her young age, she was remarkably resourceful and independent. Her curiosity about the world beyond her village drove her to plan her first solo bus journey.
HARD Q7: Change this complex dialogue to indirect speech: "Don't stand up," said the conductor. "Sit down, madam. You might fall."
Answer: The conductor told her not to stand up and advised her to sit down, warning that she might fall.
HARD Q8: Write a biographical sketch (80-100 words) of someone you admire.
Answer: Full sketch with: name, birth details, early life, achievements, qualities, and significance. Third person throughout.
HARD Q9: Explain all the changes needed when converting direct speech to indirect speech.
Answer: Changes: (1) Tense backshift: present→past, past→past perfect, will→would. (2) Pronouns: I→he/she, we→they, my→his/her. (3) Time/place: today→that day, here→there, this→that, now→then, yesterday→the previous day. (4) Reporting verb: said→told/informed/asked. (5) Remove quotation marks, add "that" for statements, use if/whether for yes-no questions.
