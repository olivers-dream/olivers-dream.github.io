Practice Exercises

EASY Q1: What does "emigrated" mean?
Answer: Left one's own country to settle in another.
EASY Q2: Choose correct verb: Everyone ___ (is/are) welcome.
Answer: is — "everyone" is treated as singular
EASY Q3: What makes a diary entry personal?
Answer: First-person pronoun (I), personal reflections, feelings, date format, informal/honest tone.
MED Q4: Fix the agreement: "Neither the students nor the teacher were present."
Answer: "Neither the students nor the teacher was present." (verb agrees with nearest subject — teacher is singular)
MED Q5: Write the opening of a diary entry about a difficult day at school.
Answer: March 1, 2026. Dear Diary, Today was absolutely terrible. Mr. Kumar returned our math tests and I...
MED Q6: Use "plaster" as both a noun and verb in sentences.
Answer: Noun: The plaster on the wall was cracking. Verb: She plastered the wound with a bandage.
HARD Q7: Write a diary entry (80-100 words) as Anne Frank on a hopeful day.
Answer: June 15, 1943. Dear Kitty, Today the sun found its way through the blackout curtains, and for once, the annex felt less like a prison. Father read poetry after dinner, and even Mother smiled. I think about the world outside — the canals, the parks, the freedom to just walk. Sometimes hope feels foolish when bombs echo in the distance, but I refuse to let go of it. As long as I can write to you, Kitty, I am not entirely alone. Yours, Anne
HARD Q8: Correct all errors: "Each of the boys have their own book. The pair of scissors are on the table."
Answer: "Each of the boys has his own book. The pair of scissors is on the table." (Each = singular; pair = singular unit)
HARD Q9: How does first-person narrative create intimacy in writing?
Answer: First person ("I") gives direct access to the narrator's thoughts, feelings, and biases. The reader becomes a confidant, creating emotional intimacy. It's subjective — we see only one perspective — which builds empathy but also unreliability.
