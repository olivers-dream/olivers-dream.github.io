Practice Exercises

EASY Q1: What does "impermanent" mean?
Answer: Not lasting forever; temporary.
EASY Q2: Change to negative: "Death is avoidable."
Answer: Death is not avoidable. / Death is unavoidable.
EASY Q3: Give the comparative and superlative: "wise"
Answer: wiser, wisest
MED Q4: Transform to complex sentence: "Buddha taught a lesson. The lesson was about death."
Answer: Buddha taught a lesson that was about death. / The lesson that Buddha taught was about death.
MED Q5: Change to interrogative: "No house has been free from death."
Answer: Has any house been free from death?
MED Q6: Write a notice (50 words) for a school meditation camp.
Answer: NOTICE — [Date] — MEDITATION CAMP — All students of classes IX and X are informed that a meditation camp will be held on [date] in the school auditorium from 9 AM to 12 PM. Interested students must register with the class teacher by [date]. — [Name], Head Boy/Head Girl
HARD Q7: Transform: Simple → Compound → Complex: "Being wise, he accepted the truth."
Answer: Compound: He was wise and he accepted the truth. Complex: Because he was wise, he accepted the truth. / Since he was wise, he accepted the truth.
HARD Q8: Correct the degrees: "Death is the most universalest truth." Also give positive and comparative degree sentences.
Answer: Correct: "Death is the most universal truth." Positive: No other truth is as universal as death. Comparative: Death is more universal than any other truth.
HARD Q9: Write a message (30-40 words) and a notice (50-60 words) about a school assembly on Buddha Jayanti.
Answer: Message: Rohan, your mother called to say the school assembly on Buddha Jayanti is at 8 AM tomorrow. Please carry your project. — Priya. Notice: NOTICE — [Date] — BUDDHA JAYANTI ASSEMBLY — A special assembly will be held to mark Buddha Jayanti on [date] at 8 AM in the main hall. Students must assemble by 7:45 AM in formal uniform. — [Name], Cultural Secretary
