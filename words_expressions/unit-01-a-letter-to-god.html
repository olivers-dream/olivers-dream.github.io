Practice Exercises

EASY Q1: What does "conscience" mean?
Answer: An inner feeling of right and wrong; moral sense.
EASY Q2: Convert to past tense: "He writes a letter."
Answer: He wrote a letter.
EASY Q3: What is a synonym for "amiable"?
Answer: Friendly, pleasant, likeable
MED Q4: Change to reported speech: He said, "I need 100 pesos."
Answer: He said that he needed 100 pesos.
MED Q5: Write the opening of a formal letter to the postmaster complaining about lost mail.
Answer: To The Postmaster, [Address], Subject: Complaint regarding lost postal mail. Sir/Madam, I wish to bring to your notice...
MED Q6: Use "at the mercy of" in a sentence.
Answer: The farmers were at the mercy of the weather. / The crop was at the mercy of the hailstorm.
HARD Q7: Explain the difference between "hope" and "expect" using examples from the story.
Answer: "Hope" implies a wish without certainty (Lencho hoped God would help). "Expect" implies confidence that something will happen (Lencho expected the full 100 pesos). Hope is emotional; expectation is cognitive.
HARD Q8: Write a formal letter from Lencho to the postmaster thanking him (assuming Lencho learned the truth).
Answer: Full formal letter format with subject, regret for accusation, gratitude for generosity, and respectful closing.
HARD Q9: Rewrite this passage in reported speech: Lencho said, "God, if you don't help me, my family will go hungry this year."
Answer: Lencho told God that if He did not help him, his family would go hungry that year.
