Practice Exercises

EASY Q1: What does "domesticated" mean?
Answer: Tamed or adapted to live with humans.
EASY Q2: Identify the conditional type: "If it rains, the match will be cancelled."
Answer: Type 1 (real/possible condition — present tense + will)
EASY Q3: Join with "but": "The otter was playful. It caused chaos on the plane."
Answer: The otter was playful but it caused chaos on the plane.
MED Q4: Complete the Type 2 conditional: "If I ___ (have) an otter, I ___ (keep) it in a garden with a pond."
Answer: If I had an otter, I would keep it in a garden with a pond.
MED Q5: Join using a subordinating conjunction: "Maxwell loved Mij. Mij was difficult to travel with."
Answer: Although Mij was difficult to travel with, Maxwell loved him. / Maxwell loved Mij even though he was difficult to travel with.
MED Q6: Write the opening paragraph of a story about finding an unusual pet.
Answer: Example: The cardboard box on my doorstep shouldn't have been moving. I approached carefully, heart thumping, and lifted the flap. Two bright eyes stared back at me from the fluffiest face I had ever seen...
HARD Q7: Write a Type 3 conditional about a missed opportunity.
Answer: If Maxwell had not gone to Iraq, he would never have discovered a new otter subspecies. / If the air hostess had not helped, the otter might have caused a panic on the plane.
HARD Q8: Write a short story (80-100 words) about an animal adventure using all three conjunction types.
Answer: The parrot escaped because the cage door was faulty, and it soared over the garden wall. Although Mrs. Sharma screamed, the bird ignored her completely. It landed on a mango tree while the neighbor's cat watched hungrily from below. Both the cat and the crow eyed the parrot, but neither could reach it. Finally, I climbed up with crackers — not only did the parrot return, but it also bit my finger in gratitude. Some rescues are thankless.
HARD Q9: Explain why Type 2 conditionals use past tense for present/future situations.
Answer: Type 2 uses past tense (subjunctive mood) to create distance from reality — it signals that the situation is hypothetical or unlikely. "If I were rich" doesn't mean past time; the past form signals unreality. This grammatical device separates the imagined world from the real one.
