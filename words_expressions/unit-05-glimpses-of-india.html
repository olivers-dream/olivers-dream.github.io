Practice Exercises

EASY Q1: Fill in the preposition: The baker arrives ___ the morning.
Answer: in the morning
EASY Q2: What does "martial" mean?
Answer: Relating to war or fighting; military in character.
EASY Q3: Identify the clause type: "which is famous for coffee"
Answer: Subordinate/relative clause (begins with "which")
MED Q4: Fill prepositions: They travelled ___ Goa ___ bus and stayed ___ a heritage hotel ___ the beach.
Answer: to Goa by bus and stayed at/in a heritage hotel near/on the beach.
MED Q5: Combine using a relative clause: "Coorg is a hill station. It is known for coffee and cardamom."
Answer: Coorg is a hill station which is known for coffee and cardamom.
MED Q6: Write 3 sentences describing a local food market using sensory details.
Answer: Example: The market buzzed with vendors calling out prices. The aroma of fresh spices — turmeric, cumin, coriander — filled the narrow lanes. Bright pyramids of mangoes and tomatoes glowed under the morning sun.
HARD Q7: Write a short travelogue paragraph (60-80 words) about a place you've visited.
Answer: The backwaters of Kerala unfolded like a green silk ribbon. Our houseboat glided past coconut groves and tiny villages where fishermen cast their nets in practiced arcs. The air carried the sweetness of jasmine mingled with woodsmoke from evening kitchens. As the sun melted into the waterline, turning the lagoon into liquid gold, I understood why they call this place God's Own Country.
HARD Q8: Explain the difference between "between" and "among" with examples.
Answer: "Between" is for two distinct items or when items are individually named: between Goa and Coorg. "Among" is for three or more in a group: among the tea gardens. Exception: "between" can be used for more than two when items are distinct: "a treaty between India, China, and Japan."
HARD Q9: Identify and classify all clauses: "The tea that grows in Assam, which receives heavy rainfall, is exported worldwide."
Answer: Main clause: "The tea is exported worldwide." Subordinate (relative) clause 1: "that grows in Assam" (defines which tea). Subordinate (relative) clause 2: "which receives heavy rainfall" (adds info about Assam — non-restrictive).
