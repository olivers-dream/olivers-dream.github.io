Practice Exercises

EASY Q1: What does "apartheid" mean?
Answer: A system of racial segregation and discrimination in South Africa.
EASY Q2: Fill in the modal: You ___ respect everyone equally. (obligation)
Answer: must / should
EASY Q3: What is the article in: "He became the first Black president"?
Answer: "the" — definite article
MED Q4: Use "emancipation" and "resilience" in one sentence.
Answer: Mandela's resilience through 27 years of prison led to the emancipation of millions of oppressed South Africans.
MED Q5: Choose the correct modal: He ___ (can/could) have escaped, but he chose to stay and fight.
Answer: could — past ability/possibility
MED Q6: Write a topic sentence for an analytical paragraph on courage.
Answer: Example: Courage is not the absence of fear but the determination to act despite it, as demonstrated by Nelson Mandela's life.
HARD Q7: Write an analytical paragraph (60-80 words) on "Freedom has responsibilities."
Answer: Freedom is not merely the absence of chains but a commitment to protecting the liberty of others. As Mandela noted, the oppressor is also not free. True freedom demands responsibility — to family, community, and nation. Without this sense of duty, freedom becomes selfishness. Therefore, every citizen must balance personal liberty with collective welfare, ensuring that freedom serves as a foundation for justice and equality.
HARD Q8: Fill in determiners: ___ inauguration was ___ historic event. ___ leaders from ___ countries attended.
Answer: The inauguration was a historic event. Many/Several leaders from various/different countries attended.
HARD Q9: Explain the difference between "shall" and "will" with examples.
Answer: "Shall" expresses determination/promise in 2nd/3rd person (You shall succeed) or suggestion in 1st person (Shall we go?). "Will" expresses simple future (I will study) or willingness. In modern English, "will" dominates for future tense.
