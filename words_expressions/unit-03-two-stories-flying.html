Practice Exercises

EASY Q1: What does "ledge" mean?
Answer: A narrow horizontal surface projecting from a wall or cliff.
EASY Q2: Change to passive: "The seagull caught the fish."
Answer: The fish was caught by the seagull.
EASY Q3: Identify the tense: "He was flying through the storm."
Answer: Past continuous tense
MED Q4: Change to active: "The plane was guided by a mysterious pilot."
Answer: A mysterious pilot guided the plane.
MED Q5: Write a descriptive sentence about a storm using 3 sensory details.
Answer: Example: The sky turned ink-black as thunder rumbled like an angry drum, and cold rain stung my face like tiny needles.
MED Q6: Fill in correct tense: By the time he landed, the storm ___ (pass).
Answer: had passed (past perfect tense)
HARD Q7: Convert this passage to passive voice: "The mother seagull brought a piece of fish. She held it near the ledge."
Answer: A piece of fish was brought by the mother seagull. It was held near the ledge (by her).
HARD Q8: Write a descriptive paragraph (50-60 words) about a frightening flight experience.
Answer: The aircraft shuddered as it plunged into the dark bank of clouds. Lightning cracked the sky like a broken mirror, illuminating the cockpit in ghostly white. My hands gripped the controls, knuckles white, as turbulence tossed us like a leaf in a hurricane. Below, the earth had vanished into an abyss of grey nothingness.
HARD Q9: Explain when to use passive voice effectively in writing.
Answer: Use passive when: (1) the doer is unknown ("The window was broken"), (2) the action matters more than the doer ("The vaccine was developed in 2020"), (3) for formal/scientific writing ("The experiment was conducted"), (4) to vary sentence structure.
