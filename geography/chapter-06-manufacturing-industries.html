Core Concepts

  • Manufacturing: producing goods in large quantities from raw materials.
  • Classification: by raw material (agro/mineral-based), size (large/small/cottage), ownership (public/private/joint/cooperative).
  • Iron & Steel: Integral plants (Bhilai, Bokaro, Jamshedpur). India 9th largest producer.
  • Cotton Textiles: largest employer after agriculture. Mumbai (Cottonopolis), Ahmedabad (Manchester of India).
  • IT & Software: Bangalore (Silicon Valley), Hyderabad, Pune, Gurugram. ~$150 billion exports.
  • Industrial pollution: air (smoke, chemicals), water (effluents), land (solid waste), noise.
  • NTPC follows strict environmental norms: installing pollution control devices, using fly ash for bricks.

Key Facts & Figures

  • Manufacturing contributes ~17% of GDP
  • Target: 25% of GDP from manufacturing

Worked Examples

Example 1: Why is Jamshedpur an ideal location for steel plant?

Expected answer: Proximity to iron ore (Noamundi), coal (Jharia), water (Subarnarekha river), transport links

Solution: Industrial location depends on proximity to raw materials, energy, water, transport, and market.

Example 2: What makes IT industry different from traditional manufacturing?

Expected answer: Requires skilled labour not raw materials; not location-bound; exports services not goods

Solution: IT is knowledge-based, can be located anywhere with connectivity. Employs educated workforce.

Practice Questions

EASY Q1: What is manufacturing?
Answer: Production of goods in large quantities from raw materials
EASY Q2: Which city is called Silicon Valley of India?
Answer: Bangalore
EASY Q3: Which is India's oldest and largest industry?
Answer: Cotton textile industry
MED Q4: Classify industries by ownership.
Answer: Public (government), Private, Joint sector (public+private), Cooperative
MED Q5: Why is cotton textile the largest industry by employment?
Answer: Labour-intensive, widespread raw material availability, long history, serves huge domestic market.
MED Q6: Name two agro-based industries.
Answer: Cotton textiles and sugar industry
HARD Q7: How does industrial pollution affect the environment?
Answer: Air: smoke, toxic gases. Water: chemical effluents kill aquatic life. Land: solid waste. Noise: health issues.
HARD Q8: Suggest measures to control industrial pollution.
Answer: Effluent treatment plants, electrostatic precipitators, green belts, recycling waste, pollution norms.
HARD Q9: Why should manufacturing contribute 25% of GDP?
Answer: Creates jobs, reduces import dependence, adds value to raw materials, drives economic growth.
