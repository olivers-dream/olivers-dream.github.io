Core Concepts

  • Agriculture employs ~2/3 of India's population.
  • Types: Subsistence (primitive & intensive), Commercial farming, Plantation.
  • Cropping Seasons: Kharif (June-Sept: rice, maize, cotton), Rabi (Oct-March: wheat, mustard, peas), Zaid (March-June: watermelon, cucumber).
  • Green Revolution: HYV seeds in wheat and rice (1960s-70s) → Punjab, Haryana became grain bowls.
  • White Revolution (Operation Flood): Gujarat (Amul) transformed India into top milk producer.
  • Major crops: Rice (W. Bengal), Wheat (UP, Punjab), Sugarcane (UP), Tea (Assam), Coffee (Karnataka), Rubber (Kerala).
  • Institutional reforms: MSP (Minimum Support Price), crop insurance, Grameen banks.

Key Facts & Figures

  • GDP share of agriculture: ~14% but employs ~43%
  • Food grains production increased 5× since 1950

Worked Examples

Example 1: What is the difference between Kharif and Rabi crops?

Expected answer: Kharif: monsoon season (June-Sept), e.g. rice. Rabi: winter season (Oct-March), e.g. wheat

Solution: Kharif sown with onset of monsoon. Rabi sown in winter, harvested in spring. Climate determines which crop grows.

Example 2: What was the Green Revolution?

Expected answer: Introduction of HYV seeds, fertilisers, irrigation in 1960s that boosted wheat and rice production

Solution: Led by M.S. Swaminathan. Punjab, Haryana, UP benefited most. Made India self-sufficient in food grains.

Practice Questions

EASY Q1: Name two Kharif crops.
Answer: Rice and maize (also cotton, jute, groundnut)
EASY Q2: Which state leads in tea production?
Answer: Assam
EASY Q3: What is the Zaid season?
Answer: Short summer season (March-June) with crops like watermelon, cucumber.
MED Q4: Explain Operation Flood.
Answer: White Revolution by Verghese Kurien (Amul). Made India the largest milk producer through dairy cooperatives.
MED Q5: What is subsistence farming?
Answer: Farming to meet family needs, not for market. Small land, traditional tools, family labour.
MED Q6: Name two institutional reforms in Indian agriculture.
Answer: Minimum Support Price (MSP) and crop insurance
HARD Q7: Why does agriculture contribute less to GDP despite employing most people?
Answer: Low productivity per worker, small land holdings, dependence on monsoon, lack of technology in many regions.
HARD Q8: Compare commercial farming and plantation agriculture.
Answer: Commercial: large-scale, capital-intensive, for market (wheat, sugarcane). Plantation: single crop on large estate, mix of agriculture and industry (tea, coffee, rubber).
HARD Q9: How has globalisation impacted Indian farmers?
Answer: Competition from imports, shift to cash crops, but also access to new markets. Small farmers struggle with low prices.
