Core Concepts

  • Mineral: naturally occurring substance with definite chemical composition and physical properties.
  • Types: Metallic (ferrous: iron, manganese; non-ferrous: copper, bauxite) and Non-metallic (limestone, mica, salt).
  • Iron ore: Odisha (largest), Jharkhand, Chhattisgarh, Goa. Types: magnetite (best, 70% Fe), hematite (60-70%).
  • Mica: Jharkhand, Bihar, Rajasthan (electrical industry).
  • Conventional energy: coal, petroleum, natural gas, hydroelectricity, nuclear.
  • Non-conventional energy: solar, wind, biogas, tidal, geothermal.
  • Coal types: Anthracite (best) > Bituminous > Lignite > Peat.
  • Petroleum: Mumbai High, Gujarat, Assam. "Liquid gold" of economy.

Key Facts & Figures

  • Anthracite > Bituminous > Lignite > Peat (energy ranking)
  • Solar energy potential in India: 20 MW per sq km

Worked Examples

Example 1: Why are minerals called non-renewable?

Expected answer: They take millions of years to form; rate of use far exceeds rate of formation

Solution: Geological processes that form minerals take millions of years. We are using them at a rate that cannot be replenished.

Example 2: Name two non-conventional energy sources.

Expected answer: Solar energy and wind energy

Solution: Non-conventional = renewable, eco-friendly. Also includes biogas, tidal, geothermal.

Practice Questions

EASY Q1: What is a mineral?
Answer: Naturally occurring substance with definite chemical composition and physical properties
EASY Q2: Name the best quality coal.
Answer: Anthracite
EASY Q3: Name two renewable energy sources.
Answer: Solar and wind energy
MED Q4: Where is iron ore found in India?
Answer: Odisha (largest), Jharkhand, Chhattisgarh, Goa, Karnataka
MED Q5: Distinguish ferrous and non-ferrous minerals.
Answer: Ferrous: contain iron (iron ore, manganese). Non-ferrous: no iron (copper, bauxite, gold).
MED Q6: Why is petroleum called liquid gold?
Answer: Extremely valuable for economy; source of fuel, plastics, chemicals. Found in limited quantities.
HARD Q7: Compare thermal and hydroelectric power.
Answer: Thermal: burns coal/gas, pollution, depleting. Hydro: uses flowing water, clean, renewable but needs dams.
HARD Q8: Why should we conserve minerals?
Answer: Non-renewable, limited stock, take millions of years to form. Need to use judiciously and develop alternatives.
HARD Q9: How can India increase use of non-conventional energy?
Answer: Government subsidies, technology development, solar parks (Gujarat/Rajasthan), wind farms (Tamil Nadu).
