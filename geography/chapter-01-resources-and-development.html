Core Concepts

  • Resources: anything in environment that satisfies needs and is technologically accessible, economically feasible, culturally acceptable.
  • Types: Natural (renewable/non-renewable), Human-made (technology, buildings), Human (knowledge, skills).
  • Resource Planning: identification → inventory → matching with national development plans.
  • Land degradation caused by mining, over-irrigation, deforestation, overgrazing.
  • Soil types in India: Alluvial, Black (Regur), Red & Yellow, Laterite, Arid, Forest & Mountain.
  • Soil conservation: contour ploughing, terrace farming, shelter belts, strip cropping.

Key Facts & Figures

  • Resource Planning = Identification + Inventory + Matching with Plans
  • Soil Erosion Control: Contour Ploughing, Terrace Farming, Shelter Belts

Worked Examples

Example 1: Why is resource planning essential for India?

Expected answer: Unequal distribution of resources across states

Solution: Some regions are rich in resources but lack development (Jharkhand), others developed but resource-poor. Planning ensures equitable access.

Example 2: Name two methods of soil conservation.

Expected answer: Contour ploughing and terrace farming

Solution: Contour ploughing: ploughing along contour lines reduces water flow. Terrace farming: steps cut on slopes reduce erosion.

Practice Questions

EASY Q1: Define a resource.
Answer: Anything in environment satisfying needs that is technologically accessible, economically feasible, culturally acceptable.
EASY Q2: Name two types of soil in India.
Answer: Alluvial soil and Black soil (Regur)
EASY Q3: What causes soil erosion?
Answer: Deforestation, overgrazing, wind, water flow
MED Q4: Why is land degradation a problem?
Answer: Reduces agricultural productivity; caused by mining, deforestation, over-irrigation, overgrazing.
MED Q5: What are the three stages of resource planning?
Answer: Identification/inventory of resources → Technology & skill development → Matching with national development plans
MED Q6: Why is alluvial soil most productive?
Answer: Rich in potash, phosphoric acid; very fertile; renewed by annual floods in river plains.
HARD Q7: Explain the link between colonialism and resource depletion.
Answer: Colonial powers exploited resources for their own benefit without conservation, causing large-scale depletion.
HARD Q8: Distinguish between Khadar and Bhangar soils.
Answer: Khadar: new alluvial, flood plains, more fertile. Bhangar: old alluvial, terrace/bluffs, less fertile, contains kankar nodules.
HARD Q9: How does shelter belt technique work?
Answer: Rows of trees planted to break wind force, reducing wind erosion in coastal and desert areas.
